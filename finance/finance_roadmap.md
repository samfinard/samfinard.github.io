# Fixed Income Finance Roadmap

## Resources
Options, Futures, and other Derivatives (7th ed.)
MIT 15.401 Finance Theory I [(class)](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/15-401-finance-theory-i-fall-2008/)

## Part I.
1. **Introduction to Fixed Income**
   - Definition of fixed income securities
   - Comparison with equity securities
   - Key features: par value, coupon rate, maturity
   - Overview of fixed income markets and their importance

2. **Types of Fixed Income Securities**
   - Treasury securities: Treasury bills, notes, and bonds
   - Swaps
   - Cap/Floor

3. **Interest Rate Fundamentals**
   - Time value of money
   - Compounding and discounting
   - Term structure of interest rates: Spot rates, forward rates
   - Yield curves: Normal, inverted, flat
   - Factors affecting interest rates

## Part II.
4. **Fixed Income Markets**
   - Primary vs. secondary markets
   - Trading platforms and mechanisms
   - Role of brokers and dealers
   - Clearing and settlement processes
   - Market participants and their roles
5. **Bond Pricing and Yields**
   - Present value and bond pricing formula
   - Yield to Maturity (YTM)
   - Current yield
   - Accrued interest and clean vs. dirty price
6. **Curve Building**
    - discount factor
    - mkt data -> curve builder -> black box curve
    - curves just give you discount factor
    - bootstrapping

## Part III.
1.  **Fixed Income Derivatives**
    - Interest rate swaps: Structure and uses
    - Options on bonds and interest rates: Types and pricing
    - Futures and forwards on fixed income securities: Uses and strategies

2.  **Reset Risk**
    - Definition and examples
    - Impact on floating-rate securities
    - Strategies to mitigate reset risk

3.  **Delta Risk**
    - Definition and examples
    - Impact on fixed income derivatives
    - Strategies to manage delta risk

4. **The Greeks**
    - **Delta:** Sensitivity to changes in the underlying asset's price
    - **Gamma:** Sensitivity of delta to changes in the underlying asset's price
    - **Vega:** Sensitivity to changes in volatility
    - **Theta:** Sensitivity to the passage of time
    - **Rho:** Sensitivity to changes in interest rates
